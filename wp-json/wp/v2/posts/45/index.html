Google+ hangouts in Gmail<\/span><\/p>\n

At Google I\/O, we announced our plans to merge <\/span>Google Talk<\/span><\/a>, <\/span>Google Messenger<\/span><\/a>, and <\/span>Google+ hangouts<\/span><\/a> into one unified, real-time messaging application. This week, <\/span>we started this effort for consumers<\/span><\/a> by integrating Google+ hangouts in Gmail to replace the old Gmail Video Chat functionality. Your customers can expect this change to roll out to Rapid Release domains in 2-3 weeks and Scheduled Release domains 2 weeks after Rapid Release. Review the <\/span>help center article<\/span><\/a> for suggestions on optimizing your network for Google+ hangouts. A Help center article with an email template to communicate this change to your customers will be published at <\/span>What's New<\/span><\/a>. <\/span><\/p>\n

Download to Google Spreadsheet from Webmaster Tools<\/span><\/p>\n

Webmaster Tools now has a new download option if your customers want to export their data directly to a Google Spreadsheet. The download option is available for most of our data heavy features, such as Crawl errors, Search queries, and Links to your site. If you enjoy digging into the data from Webmaster Tools but don\u2019t want to use Python scripts or the API, we\u2019ve <\/span>added new functionality<\/span><\/a> just for you. <\/span><\/p>\n

Have you used the new Google Groups for Business?<\/span><\/p>\n

Google recently launched a new version of Google Groups for Business, which includes better searching, customizable roles, email lists, web or Q&A forums, and collaborative inboxes. It\u2019s a great new feature both for your own business as well as your customers\u2019. Inform your customers, access user guides, and learn more by visiting <\/span>What\u2019s New<\/span><\/a>. <\/span><\/p>\n
