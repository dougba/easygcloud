Helping SMBs save money with the cloud<\/span><\/p>\n

Working with Google and Analysis Group, MIT research scientist Andrew McAfee <\/span>recently conducted a study<\/span><\/a> to understand the comparative costs of a business moving to the cloud versus remaining with a traditional on-premise IT system. In this comparison, McAfee <\/span>finds<\/span><\/a> that the typical small-to-medium-business (SMB) will significantly reduce its IT costs by doing away with its on-premise technology and moving to the cloud.<\/span> <\/span><\/p>\n

Google\u2019s data centers: an inside look<\/span><\/p>\n

While we\u2019ve shared many of our <\/span>designs and best practices<\/span><\/a>, and we\u2019ve been <\/span>publishing our efficiency data since 2008<\/span><\/a>, only a small set of employees have access to the server floor itself. On <\/span>Where the Internet lives<\/span><\/a>, you\u2019ll get a never-before-seen look at the technology, the people and the places that keep Google running. WIRED reporter <\/span>Steven Levy<\/span><\/a> spoke to the architects of our infrastructure and got an unprecedented look at its inner workings. His <\/span>new story<\/span><\/a> is an exploration of the history and evolution of our infrastructure, with a first-time-ever report from the floor of a Google data center. Read <\/span>more<\/span><\/a>.<\/span> <\/span><\/span><\/p>\n

The new compose in Gmail<\/span><\/p>\n

We're always trying to make Gmail faster and easier to use, so this week we're <\/span>introducing<\/span><\/a> a completely redesigned compose and reply experience that does just that. Your customers and users on Rapid Release domains will see the opportunity to opt-in by clicking the Compose button. They can click the \"new compose experience\" link right next to the Labels button at the top of the message. Try it out for yourself and encourage your customers and users to do so as well - they can switch back to the classic compose\/reply experience. In early 2013, this will become the default compose and reply experience in Gmail. Watch an intro <\/span>video<\/span><\/a>, review the <\/span>guide<\/span><\/a>, and learn <\/span>more<\/span><\/a>.<\/span> <\/span><\/p>\n

Controlling your computer from afar<\/span><\/p>\n

One year ago, we launched Chrome Remote Desktop in beta. From adjusting printer settings on your mom\u2019s computer to finding a lost file on your dad\u2019s laptop, Chrome Remote Desktop has made you the family hero by helping you remotely access other computers -- including your own -- via Chrome. Now, we are taking Chrome Remote Desktop out of beta, by adding some additional features. Read <\/span>more<\/span><\/a>.<\/span> <\/span><\/p>\n

One click to Docs, Sheets, and Slides<\/span><\/p>\n

Google Drive is a place where you can create, share, collaborate and keep all your stuff. Of course, there are times you want to start a new document right away\u2013say, to take notes in class or prepare a last-minute presentation for your boss. To make it even easier for you to create stuff quickly, Documents, Spreadsheets, and Presentations \u2013 now called Docs, Sheets, and Slides \u2013 are <\/span>now available<\/span><\/a> as apps in the Chrome Web Store. Once installed, shortcuts to these apps will appear when you open a new tab in Chrome.<\/span><\/p>\n
